LET'S STOP COMPANIES FROM HARVESTING YOUR DATA!

Companies can take and track your data while you are browsing the internet. They can sell this data for profit or use it to target you with advertisements. Let's change that. We have created a secure website to give you the option to take charge of your data and choose what YOU want done with it. Listed below are some ways that you can stay educated about data collection, and change the ways that companies harvest your data.

Option 1: Legislative power

Taking legislative action is the only way that we can truly stop companies from taking our data. To find your legislator, look up "Find Your Legislator" and the state that you're located in. From there, you can find a legislator's contact information. Take five minutes and email your legislator about company data collection, and why we need to put an end to it.

Here are are some tips for drafting your letter or email:

1. Be polite.
--------------------------------------

- Rude or entitled language is not likely to get a response. Your best approach is to be respectful of their position as an elected official. Also, when addressing a letter use the phrase “Representative (Last Name)” or “Senator (Last Name).”


2. Be in-district.
--------------------------------------

-While some politicians have their own issues that you both find important, they will be more receptive to people within their districts. Their job is to represent the people within their district. If you live in Kentucky, you can locate your state representative via this link: Find Your Legislator


3. Be straightforward.
--------------------------------------

-Add a subject line or letterhead to the beginning to briefly state your position. For example, “Support HB 301,” (NCRA, 2023).

-Make your arguments quickly and concisely. Long explanations take time to read, time that busy legislators and aides do not have. Plan for a single-page letter (NCRA, 2023).


4. Be unique.
--------------------------------------

-Especially at the local level, politicians represent fewer constituents. They will be more receptive to your voice. Write your letter personally, in your style. If they receive a mass of pre-written letters, they will discard them.

-Briefly share your personal connection to the issue. People connect with stories, not data (NCRA, 2023).

-Sign your letter in your own handwriting to add a more personal touch (NCRA, 2023).

Option 2: Steps to clear data

Let's clear your data. Clearing your data is a great way to delete data that apps and companies are currently storing about you. Follow the steps for each application that you want to clear your data for.

Snapchat:

Step 1: Tap the settings icon in MyProfile
Step 2: Scroll to find the "Privacy Controls" tab located in settings
Step 3: Tap the "Clear Data" tab

Instagram:

Disclaimer: This is a way to delete some data. Instagram doesn't give users the ability to clear all of it. Keep this in mind while using Instagram.

Step 1: Click on the three lines on your profile page
Step 2: Select "Settings and Privacy"
Step 3: Click on the "Accounts Center" tab
Step 4: Click on "Your information and permissions"
Step 5: Click "Search History" and choose your profile
Step 6: Delete all searches

Facebook:

Disclaimer: Facebook doesn't provide a way for its users to delete data. Keep this in mind while using the app.

Google:

Due to the massive amount of data collection that Google partakes in, there isn't a singular way to clear your data. Major steps need to be taken to delete most of the data that Google is storing. Click here to follow these steps.

TikTok:

Step 1: Tap the three bars located in the righthand corner of "My Profile"
Step 2: Click the "Settings and Privacy" tab
Step 3: Scroll to "Free up space" and click it
Step 4: Hit the "clear" icon next to cache
